A wonderful fragrant chicken biryani which is cooked in pressure cooker saving all the time consuming steps of layering and waiting for the biryani to cook in dum. Though biryani gets ready in less than 30 mins yet the flavor is not compromised at all. So next time you want to relish biryani do not procastinate head straight to the kitchen and dish out this aromatic chicken biryani.

Chicken Biryani In Pressure Cooker | How To Make Chicken Biryani In Pressure Cooker At Home

Chicken biryani in a pressure cooker all most sounds impossible. Many people are sceptical of cooking rice in pressure cooker let alone a biryani. But trust me if you follow the soaking process of rice and use the right quantity you will be able to prepare beautiful delicious chicken biryani in your own kitchen. This biryani recipe is requires less effort yet the results are awesome. This chicken biryani can be prepared for your next Dinner Party or potluck. The beauty of this chicken biryani in pressure cooker is the use of freshly grounded biryani masala. It goes well with onion-tomato raita and some kebabs.

Time 1hour // Cook Time 30minutes mins // Course(Dinner Menu, Lunch Menu, Sunday Brunch) // Cuisine(North Indian Cuisine) //serving(4)

equipment

  • Pressure Cooker

Ingredients

  • 2 cup Basmati Rice
  • 500 gms Chicken
  • 5 Green Chillies sliced in length
  • 1/2 cup Fried Onions
  • 1/2 cup Coriander Leaves
  • 1/2 cup Mint Leaves
  • 2 – 3 tblsp Ghee
  • 2 tblsp Oil
  • Salt to taste

For Marination

  • 1/4 cup Curd (Dahi)
  • 2 tsp Ginger Garlic Paste
  • 1 1/2 tsp Red Chilli Powder
  • 1 1/2 tsp Coriander Powder
  • 1/4 tsp Turmeric Powder
  • 1 tsp Garam Masala
  • 1 1/2 tsp Salt
  • Juice of 1/2 Lemon
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped Mint Leaves
  • 1/2 cup Fried Onions (Barista)
  • 1/2 cup finely Coriander Leaves

For Biryani Masala Powder

  • 3 tblsp Whole Coriander Seeds (Sabut Dhania)
  • 3 Cloves (Laung) 3
  • 1″ inch Cinnamon 1
  • 1 tsp Cumin Seeds (Jeera)
  • 1 whole Brown Cardamom (Moti Elacihi)
  • 2 – 3 blades Mace (Jiavitri) 1
  • 1 Star Anise (Chakri Phool)
  • 4 Green Cardamom (Chotti Elacihi)
  • 2 tsp Fennel Seeds (Saunf)
  • 1/4 tsp Nutmeg Powder (jaiphal)
  • 4 – 5 Black Stone Flower

Spiced Water

  • 3 Cups Water
  • 2 Bay leaves
  • 1 Brown Cardamom (Moti Elaichi)
  • 1 tsp Cumin Seeds (Jeera)
  • 2 Green Cardamom (Choti Elacihi)
  • 2 Cloves (Laung)

How to Make Chicken Biryani In Pressure Cooker

Soaking Rice

Wash and rinse the basmati rice in water 3 – 4 times till the water becomes clear. Soak for 20 minutes. It is Important to soak only and only for 20 mins. As the water quantity used to make this biryani in pressure cooker has to be precise. Any extra soaking would make the rice mushy and soft. So just after 20 mins drain out the water and keep the rice aside. You should soak the rice only when you begin to prepare the chicken. This should take roughly 20 mins and by then the rice will be soaked enough.

Marinating the chicken

In a bowl add chicken pieces and add all the ingredients given under “for marination”. Mix well and keep it aside for minimum for 30 mins and maximum 2 hours.

Making Biryani Masala

Meanwhile, dry roast on low flame all the masalas under the “Biryani Masala Powder” and keep it aside. Once cool grind to a fine paste. It is possible to use readymade Biryani Masala too. There are many popular brands like Tata Sampam and Shan Biryani Masala. You can use any. But a freshly gounded biryani takes it to the next level.

Cooking

  • In a pressure cooker, add the ghee and oil and when hot, add the marinated chicken pieces. Let the chicken cook and simmer on medium flame in the ghee for 7 – 10 mins. Keep flipping often so that the masala does not get burnt. Incase the masala begins to stick at the bottom add some1/4 cup of water and continue cooking the chicken. Soon the chicken will be 90% done.

  • Mean while in a pan boil 3 cups of water with all the whole spices under the spiced water section. You can skip this step and add all the whole spices in the beginning before you add the marinated chicken. But do add HOT water only and not room temperature. This makes a lot of difference.
  • Once the chicken is ready, add the hot boiling water and the soaked rice. Add 2 tblsp of biryani masala and salt at this stage and with a very light hand give the rice a stir. Do not apply pressure or the rice will break.
  • Let the rice cook till the water begins to boil.
  • Once the water begins to bubble add some chopped mint leaves, coriander leaves, barista (fried onions) and put the lid.
  • Cook the rice for just 1 whistle and then switch off the flame and let the steam release naturally. In case you soaked the rice longer than 20 mins in that case after 1 whistle immediately release the pressure from the cooker. This will ensure that the rice does not get mushy or over cooked.
  • Once the steam is out fluff up the rice with a fork with a gentle hand and serve with raita and salan.
  • Your chicken biryani in pressure cooker is ready.

Side dish Options – What To Serve With Chicken Biryani

  • Curd: Serve with Pineapple Raita, Onion-Tomato Raita.
  • Salad: Sirke Wale Pyaaz.
  • Side Dish: Chicken 65, Salan, Chicken Gravy.

You can use either chicken or mutton to make this dish. Go ahead and try it. It is a wonderfully light chicken recipe because there is no use of heavy cream or cashewnuts or almonds.

CHICKEN BHUNA MASALA

Chicken bhuna masala as the names suggest is made by bhunoeing or sauteing the masala on low flame for a long time to get that perfect flavor and texture. A simple and easy to make. Serve chicken bhuna masala with naan or paratha makes a sumptuous treat. It is finger licking delicious.

Time: 15mins // Cook Time: 1hour // Cuisine(Indian) //serving(4)

equipment

  • Kadai / Pan

Main Ingredients

  • Chicken
  • Onion
  • Tomato
  • Kasoori Methi (Dried Fenugreek Leaves)
  • Curd

Ingredients

  • 500 gms Chicken On Bone
  • 4 – 5 large Onions roughly chopped
  • 3 medium sized Tomatoes roughly chopped
  • 2-3 tblsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 3-4 green chilies – roughly chopped
  • 2 tsp Kashmiri Chilli Powder for color
  • 1 tsp Red Chilly Powder
  • 2 tsp Coriander Powder
  • 1 tsp Black Salt (Kala Namak)
  • 1/2 Amchur Powder (Dry Mango Powder)
  • 1 tsp roasted Jeera Powder (Cumin Seeds)
  • 2 tsp Kasoori Methi Powder (Dry Fenugreek Leaves)
  • 1/4 cup beaten Curd
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 – 3 tblsp Ghee

Ingredients For Garam Masala

  • 1 tblsp Black Pepper Corns (Kali Mirch)
  • 1 tblsp Green Cardamoms (Choti Elaichi)
  • 1 tsp Jeera (Cumin Seeds)
  • 6 – 7 Cloves (Laung)
  • 4-5 Mace Blades
  • 1 tblsp Black Cardamoms (Moti Elaichi)
  • 5 Bay Leaves
  • 2 – 3 Cinnamon Sticks

For garnishing

  • 3 – 4 Slit Green Chilies for garnishing
  • 2 tblsp finely chopped Coriander Leaves

How to Make Fresh Garam Masala

  • Lightly roast all ingredients in a pan over medium heat. Once the aroma starts to come out, put the ingredients in a grinding bowl. Grind to a fine powder. Keep in a sterilised bottle for further use.
  • In a heavy botom pan / kadai, heat the ghee/oil. Now add roughly chopped onions and saute them till golden brown in color. This will take 10 – 15 mins. Do not fry on high flame. Let the onions saute on medium flame and sir it occasionally. Make sure the onions are brown but not burnt. Otherwise the curry will have bitter taste.
  • Now add ginger-garlic paste and saute further till the raw smell of garlic and ginger disappears.
  • Now add the kashmiri red chilly powder. Stir it in the ghee to let it release its color.
  • Immediately add tomato puree, green chilies and salt.
  • Continue to saute on high heat until tomato puree gets cooked. Add the dry masalas and cook till oil begins to separate.
  • Lower down the heat, add beaten smooth curd and keep stirring till curd gets into the masala properly.
  • Cover and cook till more oil begins to separate from the masala.
  • If the masala begins to stick to the base of the kadai then keep on splashing some water.
  • Now add washed chicken pieces and begin to cook on high heat for 7-8 mins.
  • Add 2 tsp freshly garam masala and add little water just enough to avoid sticking of the masala. Keep the remaining garam masala for some other time. You can use it in korma, curry, and kebabs.
  • Cover and cook on low flame.
  • Keep stirring in between. Cook until done. Should take 10 -1 2 mins.
  • Switch off the flame and transfer the chicken to the serving bowl.
  • Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and slit green chilies.
  • Your bhuna chicken masala is ready.

What To Serve With Bhuna Chicken Masala

  • Main Course: Serve with any Kebab, Dal Makhani, Vegetable Biryani.
  • Bread: Serve it with Plain Naan, Garlic Naan, Paratha or Roti.
  • Sides: Hari Chutney, Lacche Wale Pyaz.

MURGH DO PYAAZA RECIPE

Murgh Do Pyaaza is very special kind of chicken dish in which the onion is used in two types. Learn how to make chicken do pyaaza.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 kg Chicken On Bone
  • 1 1/2 tblsp Ginger Garlic Paste
  • 1 tblsp Red chilli Powder
  • Salt to Taste

For the curry

  • 1 large onions chopped
  • 2 big tomatoes
  • 1 1/2 tsp ginger garlic paste
  • 1/4 cup beaten curd
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 brown Cardamom
  • 3 Cloves
  • 5 – 6 Black peppercorns
  • 1/4 ” Cinnamom Stick
  • 1 tsp Red chili powder
  • 1 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 1/2 tsp corainder powder
  • 1 1/2 tsp cumin powder
  • 2 tsp Garam Masala
  • 6 – 7 pearl onions or 1 large onion chopped in big cubes
  • 1/2 cup water (or as needed)
  • 2 tblsp Ghee or Oil
  • Salt To Taste

For Garnish

  • 3 – 4 long ginger juliennes
  • 1 tblsp chopped coriander leaves

How to make murg do pyaaz

  1. We start the chicken preparation by first marinating the chicken for 3 – 4 hours.
  2. For Marination – clean and dry the chicken and cut into medium size pieces.
  3. ginger-garlic paste, red chilly and salt on all the chicken pieces. Cover it and keep it aside for 3 – 4 hours in fridge.
  4. In a kadhai , heat the ghee or oil what ever you are using.Now add the bay leaf and then add the ginger garlic paste. Fry it till it gets nice brown. Then add the chopped onions. Cook then till they are also brown. Make sure not to burn the onions.
  5. Now add the tomato puree and salt and cook till all the raw smell disappears and the oil separates from the paste.
  6. When the oil separates add the the turmeric powder, red chilly powder and the beaten curd. Stir fry till all the curd is very well incorporated in the gravy. This should take 5 – 10 minutes.
  7. Now add all the dry spices – coriander powder, cumin powder and half of garam masala. Also add the chicken pieces and the water.
  8. Stir everything well and add cover the kadhai and let the chicken cook on low flame for 10 minutes.
  9. Stir occasionally as not to burn the paste. Do not over cook the chicken or it will become chewy.
  10. When chicken is done add the rest of the garam masala.
  11. Take out the chicken in a serving bowl and garnish with green coriander and ginger julienne.

INDIAN CHILLI CHICKEN RECIPE

Indian Chilli chicken is a very popular chicken snack eaten in India. Here is the recipe for dry chilli chicken.

Ingredients

  • 250 gms boneless chicken breast (cut into 1 inch cubes)
  • 1 egg white
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 4 tsp red chilly sauce
  • 2 -3 tsp cornflour
  • 1 tblsp soy sauce
  • 2 tblsp tomato ketchup
  • 1 tsp white vinegar
  • 2 tblsp oil
  • 1/2 tsp ginger paste
  • 1 tsp garlic paste
  • 1 big onion sliced long
  • 1/2 cup capsicum (cut into big cubes)
  • 1 -2 green chillies
  • Green corainder and green onion for garnish
  • Oil for frying

How to make Indian chili chicken

  1. Marinate the chicken pieces in egg white, 1 tsp salt, 2 tsp red chilly sauce and 2 -3 tsp cornflour. Mix well and keep for 10 – 15 minutes.
  2. In a bowl mix 2 tsp red chilly sauce, soy sauce, tomato ketchup and white vinegar. Keep aside for later use.
  3. Now heat oil for frying in a kadai/wok and fry the chicken pieces separately for 2 -3 minutes. Do not over fry otherwise the chicken will get hard. Take them out on a paper tissue.
  4. In a separate pan heat 2 tblsp oil and add garlic and ginger paste. Fry till golden color and then add sliced big onion and capsicum cubes and slit green chillies. Stir fry on high flame for 2 -3 minutes.Do not over cook the vegetables. The vegetables should be crunchy.
  5. Now lower the flame and add the sauce mixture and 1/4 cup of water.Mix well.
  6. When slightly thick and dry, shut the flame and mve it in a serving platter.
  7. Garnish with green chopped corainder and green onion .Serve hot.

Note

  1. Be careful with salt as the soy sauce also has salt.
  2. Cook the vegetables on high flame to keep their crunchiness.

BEANS ALOO KI SABZI

Beans aloo sabzi recipe is one of the simplest and easiest way to add greens to any meal. Be it lunch or dinner. This sabzi is versatile and goes well with any dal, curry or even with plain chapati and curd. This beans potato sabzi does not use any onion, garlic or tomato. Its totally sattvic sabzi. It is highly nutritious and healthy.

Equipment

  • Kadhai
  • Pan

Main Ingredients

  • French Beans
  • Potatoes
  • Indian Spices

Ingredients

  • 250 gms French Beans washed and chopped
  • 1 big Potato (Aloo) cut in small cubes
  • 1 tblsp Oil
  • 1 tsp Cumin Seeds (Jeera)
  • 1 tsp Red Chilli Powder (Lal Mirch)
  • 1 tsp Coriander Powder (Dhaniya Powder)
  • 1 tsp Turmeric Powder (Haldi)
  • 1/2 tsp Garam Masala
  • Salt as per taste

How to Make Beans Aloo Ki Sabzi

  1. Heat oil in a kadhai and add cumin seeds.
  2. When the seeds begin to sputter, add cubed potatoes.
  3. Cover and cook the potato soft and 3/4th done.
  4. Now add turmeric powder and then immediately add beans and salt . Do not add any dry masala at this stage as they might get burnt.
  5. Again cover and cook till the beans are properly cooked. If you feel that the sabji is sticking to the bottom of the kadhai then sprinkle 1 tblsp of water and cover and cook till the beans and aloo are done.
  6. Keep Stirring occasionally.
  7. When the beans are completely done add red chilly powder, coriander powder and garam masala.
  8. Mix everything well, cover and let it cook for another 2mins for the beans to take in the masala. You can leave it till the veggies are done or you can stir fry it further to make it crunchy.
  9. Your simple beans aloo sabzi is ready…

What To Serve With Beans Aloo Sabzi

Beans aloo tastes great with any kind of Dal – Yellow Moong Dal, Green Moong Dal, Kadhi, Rajma Curry or even with just plain raita.

For Meal Planning

You can wash, dry and cut the beans and store it in a Ziplock bags for over a week. Another great tip is to use Boiled Potatoes in case you are in a hurry. This will save lots of time. Or you can fry the potatoes in a big batch and freeze them and use them as per the dish.

DAL MAKHANI WITHOUT ONION GARLIC RESTAURANT STYLE

Dal Makhani without onion garlic restaurant style is a super easy rustic recipe. It is unbelievable that restaurant style dal makhani can made at home so easily and does not require too many spices. People always wonder why is Dal Makhani is called Makhani. The reason being in the generous use of butter and cream and cooking on low flame for over an hour. In restaurants dal makhani simmers on the flame for hours before it is served. Simmering the dal makhani on low flame gives it a creamy “makhamali” texture just like “Makhaan” (butter) hence the name. This extra richness adds lots and lots of flavor and creaminess to the Dal Makhani. Hence the name “Makhani”.

EQUIPMENT

  • Pressure Cooker / Insta Pot
  • Kadai / Heavy Bottom Kadai

MAIN INGREDIENTS

  • Urad Dal (Whole Black Gram)
  • Rajma (Red Kidney Beans)
  • Cream
  • Butter
  • Kasoori Methi

INGREDIENTS

  • 200 gms Black Urad Dal
  • 50 gms Rajma (Kidney Beans)
  • 4 medium size Tomatoes
  • 2 – 3 Green Chillies
  • 1 tsp Kashmiri Chilli Powder (For color)
  • 1 tsp Chilli Powder
  • 1 Badi Elaichi (Brown Cardamom)
  • 1 1/2 tsp Jeera (Cumin Seeds)
  • 1 tbslp Kasuri Methi (Dried Fenugreek Leaves)
  • 1 tsp Garam Masala
  • 2 tblsp Coriander Leaves (Hara Dhania)
  • 4 tblsp Ghee
  • 100 gms Amul Butter
  • 100 ml Fresh Cream
  • Salt to taste

How to Make Dal Makhani without Onion Garlic

  1. Soaking the dal – Wash and soak the urad dal and rajma overnight. In case you forget to soak overnight or for 8 hours than you can wash and soak the dal and rajma in hot water for 1 – 2 hour and keep it covered.
  2. Next day, drain the water and in a pressure cooker add the dal, rajma, 1 moti elaichi (Black cardamom), salt and 5 cups of water.
  3. Now cook the dal for 7 – 8 whistles and then lower the flame and cook for 10 mins. Then switch off the flame and let the pressure release naturally.
  4. When you will open the pressure cooker the dal will be very soft to touch.
  5. Now make fine puree of tomatoes and green chillies.
  6. In a separate kadai or deep pan, add the ghee and heat it.
  7. Add the jeera. When the jeera begins to crackle add the Kashmiri Red Chilly Powder. Just stir it for few seconds and then add the tomato puree. Make sure the kashmiri red chilly powder does not get burnt.
  8. Let the puree cook for sometime till ghee begins to separate from the sides.
  9. Now add garam masala, red chilly powder and the dal.
  10. Stir it well and mix well till all the puree and dal is mixed properly. While mixing the dal mash it with the back of your ladle. This will give the dal a creamy texture.
  11. Now lower the flame and add 50 gms of butter and let it melt in the dal properly. Then cover the pan with a lid and let it simmer for 20 – 25 mins on low flame.
  12. In between stir the dal occasionally so that the dal does not stick on the bottom.
  13. If the dal is very thick then add 1/2 cup of warm water. Remember if ever you have to add water make sure it is hot / warm. Never use water at room temparature.
  14. Adjust the consistency and then add crushed kasoori methi and add the remaning 50 gms of butter. Let the butter get melted in the dal again.
  15. Again cover the pan and let the dal cook for another 15 – 20 mins. Stir it occasionally mashing the dal with the back of the ladle.
  16. By now the dal will get a creamy red color. Taste the salt and adjust the seasoning. Switch off the flame.
  17. Now add the cream and mix thoroughly in the dal. Do not cook the dal after adding the cream. The cream might cuddle and make the dal with white spots. Not a beautiful sight.
  18. Finally dish out the dal to a serving bowl. Garnish with some crushed fenugreek leaves and a tablespoon of butter.
  19. A very simple easy Dal Makhani with very few ingredients. Enjoy your favorite dal makhani without onion garlic restaurant style at home.

What To Serve With Dal Makhani

  • You can serve the dal makahani with Tandoori Roti, Naan, Ulte Tawa Ka Parantha, Amritsari Chur Chur Naan, Laccha Paratha.
  • Along with it Sirke Wale Pyaz, Hari Chutney and Masala Papad.
  • For rice you can serve Veg Biryani, Peas Pulao or Jeera Rice.
  • For side dish you can serve – Shahi Paneer Without Onion Garlic, Paneer Butter Masala, Gobhi Masala, Dum Aloo, Jeera Aloo Restuarant Style.

STUFFED TOMATO WITH GRAVY RECIPE

Stuffed Tomato with gravy is a slightly lengthy recipe but worth every effort and time. It is a gourmet dish where the tartness of the tomato is fantastically balanced by the sweet paneer-aloo filling in the tomato along the backdrop of spicy tomato gravy. Earlier this dish was reserved for special occasions, but with people trying out new dishes and recipes these days, it seems perfect for an afternoon lunch or even dinner. So lets learn more how to make bharwan tamatar curry.

  • Serves : 3- 4
  • Total Time: 1 1/2 hr

INGREDIENTS

  • For Filling
  • 6- 7 small Tomatoes red, ripe and firm
  • 2 medium Boiled mashed Potatoes
  • 100 gms grated Paneer (Cottage Cheese)
  • 1 small chopped Onion
  • 1 tsp finely chopped Coriander Leaves
  • 1 – 2 Green Chilly finely chopped
  • 1 tsp finely chopped Ginger
  • 1/2 tsp Jeera (Cumin Seeds)
  • 1/2 tsp Coriander Powder
  • 1/2 tsp Garam Masala
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder (Haldi Powder)
  • 1 tsp Amchoor (Dry Mango Powder)
  • 15 – 20 Kishmish (Optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tsp Oil
  • For the gravy:
  • 1 medium Onion finely grated
  • 1/2 inch Ginger finely grated
  • 2-3 cloves Garlic grated
  • 2 Tomatoes
  • Pulp of scooped tomatoes, saved from above tomatoes
  • 1 Green Chilly
  • 10 – 12 Kaju (Cashew Nuts)
  • 1 tsp Cumin Seeds (Jeera)
  • 1 tsp Kashmiri Chilly Powder
  • 1/2 tsp Chilli Powder
  • 1 tsp Coriander Powder
  • 1/2 tsp Garam Masala
  • 1/2 tsp kasoori Methi
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 cup Water
  • 2 tbsp Cream / Malai
  • 1 tblsp Cream for garnishing
  • 1 tblsp finely chopped coriander leaves

How to make bharwan tamatar curry

To stuff tomatoes

  • Wash and dry the tomatoes. Slice thin tops off the tomatoes.
  • Scoop out tomato flesh/seeds, using a potato peeler. Save the flesh for gravy.
  • Heat oil in a pan, add the jeera when the jeera begins to sputter add the chopped onion. when the onions become pink add the chopped ginger and green chillies.
  • Add the dry masalas. Mix well.
  • Add the mashed potatoes and grated paneer and salt.
  • Mix well and saute for 1 – 2 mins. Then add the chopped coriander leaves.
  • Keep it aside and let it cool.
  • Once the masala gets cool stuff keep 1 tablespoon of the aloo paneer masala aside for later use.
  • Then stuff each tomato with this mixture. It is important to to stuff the filling properly in the tomatoes. Press hard to all nooks of the tomato.
  • If you want you can even keep the tomatoes lids on the top and close with the tooth pick.
  • Now take a flat pan, add some oil.
  • When the oil heats up, lower the flame and carefully place the stuffed tomatoes in the oil.
  • Keep flipping the tomatoes every few minutes.
  • The tomato skin should get soft but should not loose its shape.
  • Take them out and keep aside.

To make the gravy

  • Make a fine paste of Tomatoes, tomato pulp, cashewnuts and green chilly.
  • Heat oil in a pan, add cumin seeds, let the seeds splutter.
  • Add onion, ginger and garlic fry until golden to dark brown but Not burnt.
  • Add all the powder spices and stir for 10 seconds to release the flavours.
  • Add tomato paste and let it cook till the oil separates.
  • Now add the 1 tblsp aloo paneer mixture kept aside.
  • Finally tip in the cream and mix well
  • Sprinkle crushed kasoori methi on the top.
  • Pour in 1/2 cup water and bring to boil and simmer for a couple of minutes.

To Serve

  • First Way: Place the tomatoes on a flat serving dish and then pour gravy on top of the tomatoes.
  • Second Way: Lay a bed of the thick tomato gravy and then place the stuffed tomatoes on it.
  • Tomatoes must not be overcooked or they will collapsed.
  • You can garnish them with coriander leaves and cream.
  • Serve hot with chapati or naan.
  • Your spicy red masala stuffed tomatoes with gravy is ready.

NO ONION GARLIC PALAK PANEER

Here is no onion garlic palak paneer restaurant style. This satvik or Jain palak paneer recipe is amazingly easy and simple to prepare. It can be prepared in less than 30 mins. Make this palak paneer restaurant style and dazzle your family members, friends and guests with your culinary skils. Lets learn how to make palak paneer without onion garlic. This palak paneer has silky smooth curry. This curry is a feast for the eyes. The white paneer cubes dipped in green palak curry is an elegant sight.

KEYWORD

  • Jain Style Palak Paneer, No Onion Garlic, Palak Paneer without Onion Garlic.
  • Restaurant Style Palak Paneer, Satvik Recipes.

INGREDIANTS

  • 500 gms Clean Palak (Spinach)
  • 200 gms Paneer (Cottage Cheese)
  • 1/2 tsp Sugar
  • 2 – 3 medium Tomatoes (Tamatar)
  • 2 – 3 Green Chilly (Hari Mirch)
  • 1″ inch Ginger (Adrak)
  • 1 1/2 tblsp Cahewnuts (Kaju)
  • 2 tblsp Ghee / Oil
  • 1 Taj Patta
  • 1 – 2 Black Cardamom (Badi Elaichi)
  • 1/4 tsp Hing (Asafoetida)
  • 1 tsp Jeera (Cumin Seeds)
  • 1 tsp Garam Masala
  • 1 tsp Coriander Powder
  • 1/2 tsp Red Chilly Powder
  • 1/2 cup Cream / Beaten Malai
  • Salt To Taste
  • Tempering
  • 1 tblsp Butter
  • 1/2 tsp Kashmiri Red Chilly
  • Garnish
  • 1/2 tsp Kasoori Methi (Dried Fenugreek Leaves)
  • 1 tblsp Fresh Amul Cream

How to make no onion garlic palak paneer

  1. Take around 750 – 900 gms of palak to get around 500 gms of clean spinach leaves.
  2. Clean the spinach and wash it 3 -4 times to remove all the sand in it.
  3. Boil the spinach in little water and 1/2 tsp sugar for 5 – 10 mins. Immediately switch off the flame and put it in cold water to stop the cooking of spinach. This will keep the color of the spinach green. Definitely an extra step but helps in maintaining the green color of palak.
  4. Once the palak cools down, make a puree of it. Keep aside.
  5. Cut the paneer in 1 inch cubes.
  6. In a pan, add oil and shallow fry cubes of paneer on medium flame till golden brown. Just remmeber that frying paneer is optional. You can use raw paneer as it is.
  7. In a blender jar make puree of tomatoes, green chilly, ginger and chopped cashewnuts. This puree should be smooth and no pieces of cashewnuts should be there.
  8. In a pan heat the ghee or oil and add cumin seeds.
  9. When the cumin seeds begin to crackle add hing and tej patta and badi elaichi.
  10. After 30 secs add the tomato-cashewnut puree.
  11. Let it cook for 5 mins or till the oil separates.
  12. Now add the dry masalas – coriander powder, red chilly powder, garam masala and salt. Mix well.
  13. Add the puree and stir well. Add 2 tablespoon of water and cover the pan, lower the flame and let it cook for 10 mins. Open and stir occasionally to ensure that palak does not get burnt.
  14. Now add the cream and mix well.
  15. Add cubed paneer (fried or raw).
  16. Cover and let the palak cook for 2 more minutes and then switch off the flame.
  17. Take out the palak paneer in a serving dish.
  18. In a small tpan heat the butter and as soon as the butter melts add the kashmiri red chilly powder and immediately pour it over the palak paneer.
  19. Garish with crushed kasori methi and cream.
  20. Your satvik palak paneer is ready to be served.

What To Serve With Palak Paneer

Palak paneer goes very well with tandoori roti, laccha paratha, arhar dal, dal makhani, naan or jeera rice.

How To Remove Bitterness From Palak

Sometimes the palak has a bitter taste. If you know in advance that palak in your region is generally bitter than while make puree of boiled palak, add 30 – 50 grams of coriander (fresh hara dhania) in it. Yes you heard it correct fresh coriander leaves. This adds sweetness to the spinach curry. But if you realize that palak is bitter after it has been cooked then add few drops of lemon to it. That should work.

How Long Can you Store / Freeze Palak Paneer

You can store palak paneer in refrigerator for 2 days and in freezer for a month.

Reheating Tips of Palak Paneer

To reheat, you can heat it in microwave if you had stored in a microwave proof container. Else you can heat 2 tblsp of water in a pan and you can take whole or part of cooked palak paneer in it. Let it simmer on low flame for 5 – 10 mins covered with a lid. Also you can give fresh tadka to it again.

BENGALI STYLE TOMATO CHUTNEY RECIPE

Here is a recipe for bengali style tomato chutney. A bong style spicy tangy tomato chutney. In bengali households tomato chutney is a permanent feature on the dining table. They love to have this sweet sour tomato chutney with every thing. Especially with bengali khichudi and begun bhaja. Each family has its own combination. Some add raw mango or khajur (dates) and some even aam papad. Some people add a dash of jaggery or sugar. Lets learn how to make bengali style tomato chutney.
  • Serves : 3- 4
  • Total Time: 30 mins

INGREDIENTS

  • 6 medium tomatoes (slightly tart)
  • 2″ inch Ginger grated
  • 2 Whole Dry Red Chilli
  • 1/2 cup Sugar (reduce if using sweet tomatoes)
  • 1 tsp Panch Phoran (Check foot note for recipe)
  • A pinch of Salt
  • A handful of Raisins (Khishmish)
  • 1 tsp Mustard Oil

How To Make Bengali Tomato Chutney

  • In a kadai/pan, heat the mustard oil and add the dry whole red chillies and panch phoran.
  • When the seeds begin to sputter add the chopped tomatoes and saute on high flame for 5 mins. Keep stiring it constantly.
  • When the tomatoes will begin to soften and release water.
  • When the tomatoes become mushy add grated ginger and stir fry for a minute more.
  • Then add sugar, salt and raisins. Cover the tomatoes and let them cook till the water evaporates and tomatoes get tender.
  • Add chopped fresh coriander for extra freshness.
  • Once done transfer it to a bowl and let it cool down.
  • Your bengali tomato chutney with raisins is ready to be served with a benagli meal.

SWEET FRUIT CHUTNEY RECIPE

Sweet fruit chutney is a delightful blend of ripe fruits, sugar, and aromatic spices. It combines the tanginess of apples or mangoes with the sweetness of dates or raisins, creating a rich, flavorful condiment. This chutney pairs perfectly with savory dishes, enhancing their taste with its sweet and spicy notes. Easy to make and versatile, it adds a burst of flavor to snacks and meals alike.

INGREDIANTS

  • 120 grams whole Amchoor (dry mango slices)
  • 4 teacups Water
  • 180 to 200 grams Sugar (Cheeni) (1 1/4 or 1 1/2 cup)
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground spices
  • 1/2 teaspoon Black Salt (Kala namak)
  • 1 teaspoon white Cumin powder
  • Salt (Namak) to taste
  • 2 teaspoons Red chili pepper (Lal Mirchi)
  • 1 teaspoon dry Ginger (Adrak) powder
  • 60 grams fresh Ginger (Adrak), sliced (2 tsps)
  • 4 Bananas (Kela)
  • 4tsps Raisins (Kishmish)

How to make sweet fruit chutney

  1. Soak the whole amchoor in water overnight.
  2. Cook it in the same water until tender.
  3. Cool and pass through a sieve.
  4. Add to it salt, chilli powder, white cumin powder, dry ginger, fresh ginger slices and black salt dissolved in a little water and mix well.
  5. Add sliced bananas and raisins soaked in water and mix.
  6. Serve at dinner or lunch.

ONION CHUTNEY RECIPE

To make onion chutney, sauté chopped onions until golden. Add dried red chilies, garlic, and tamarind, then cook briefly. Cool and blend with salt and water until smooth. In the same pan, prepare tempering with mustard seeds, urad dal, and curry leaves. Pour the tempering over the chutney. Serve with dosa, idli, or rice.

INGREDIANTS

  • cup peeled onions
  • 6 -7 flakes of garlic
  • 2 tbs channa daal (Bengal Gram)
  • 5 red chilies or as per taste
  • Salt As Per Taste
  • 1tsp mustard seeds
  • curry Leaves
  • little tamarind (Imli)

How to make onion chutney (pyaaz ki chutney)

  1. Heat oil, fry chilies, daal, garlic and the onions, till golden brown.
  2. Grind with tamarind and salt.
  3. Splutter mustard seeds and curry leaves, in oil and pour on top.
  4. Serve with paratha or puri.

DOMINOS STYLE GARLIC BREAD

Craving for Dominos style garlic bread… Do not look further.. Here is an easy homemade delicious Dominos style garlic bread from scratch. Everybody loves them. Delicious, cheesy garlic bread made in dominos style, sprinkled with dried herbs baked to perfection. Total bliss…

PREP TIME

  • 2hours hrs

COURSE

  • Evening Snacks, Fast Food, Lunch Box, Sunday Special

EQUIPMENT

  • OTG (Oven)
  • Deep Bowl
  • Brush

MAIN INGREDIANTS

  • Maida (All Purpose Flour)
  • Yeast
  • Sugar
  • Pizza Seasoning
  • Butter
  • Cheese

KEYWORD

Cheesy Dominos Style Garlic Bread, Homemade Garlic Bread Dominos Style>

INGREDIANTS

  • 2 cup Maida
  • 2 tblsp Butter / Oil
  • 1 tsp Sugar
  • 1 tsp Salt
  • 1 tsp Yeast
  • 2 tblsp Pizza Seasoning
  • 1 cup Mozerella Cheese
  • 1 cup Warm Water
  • 2 tblsp finley minced Garlic
  • 2 tblsp Butter

How to Make Dominos Style Garlic Bread Homemade

  1. Take 1/2 cup of water in a microwave proof bowl, add sugar and heat it for 30 secs. Take it out and dissolve the sugar with the spoon.
  2. This will bring down the temperature down. Check the temperature it should be lukewarm. Add yeast and stir once and then cover and keep aside for 10 mins and let the yeast bloom. After 10 mins if the yeast does not bloom then start again. Discard this mixture. Check the picture below to see how the yeast should bloom.
  3. Yeast Bloom
  4. In a mixing bowl mix together maida, salt, oil, minced garlic and add the yeast mixture.
  5. Start to knead and knead for good 8 – 10 minutes.This results in beautiful and soft texture of bread. Do escape from that.
  6. Kneading Pizza Dough
  7. Then make a ball of it and smear oil on the dough. Cover it with a plate or a cotton cloth and keep aside.
  8. Keep it aside for 1 to 2 hour until dough will rise and becomes double in volume. This time may vary depending upon the weather in your location.
  9. After 1 hour or when the dough becomes double. Punch the dough and knead again for 1 min.
  10. Now take the dough and cut into 2 equal size balls .
  11. At this stage Preheat oven at 200 degrees for 10 minutes.
  12. Now sprinkle some maida on counter and roll out a roti.
  13. Now apply butter literally on the edges of the roti.
  14. Sprinkle a liberal amount of grated mozerella on half the roti and then flip the other half end. Fold the roti to resemble semicircle.
  15. Seal the corners well using fork or just fingers to pinch the ends.
  16. Set aside for the dough to raise for another 10 minutes.
  17. Apply lots of melted butter on the top and sprinkle pizza seasoning.
  18. Make long slits on the dough.
  19. Place the garlic bread on the tray and bake the garlic bread at 200 degree for at least 15 minutes or till the top gets a nice golden crust. Apply butter immediately. This gives a soft texture.
  20. Take it out and enjoy with a nice dip. Enjoy Hot straight from the oven… let the cheese ooze out.

What To Serve With Cheesy Garlic Bread Dominos Style

  • You can serve the garlic bread with cheese dip and cold drinks.

Storage Tips

  • You can easily store the garlic bread in an airtight box in fridge. You can store it for 2 – 3 days.

Reheating Tips

  • Microwave: You can reheat the garlic bread in a the microwave for 2 – 3 mins.

GARLIC BREAD WITH BREAD SLICES AT HOME

Here is an easy cheese garlic bread with bread slices. This garlic bread is very tasty and is a perfect snacks recipe. This homemade cheese garlic bread is made with regular bread and it tastes awesome with soups and pasta. Minced fresh garlic is the key to make these flavor packed crusty yummy slices. Lets learn how to make garlic cheese bread at home.

PREP TIME

  • 10minutes

COOKING TIME

  • 10minutes

TOTAL TIME

  • 20minutes

COURSE

  • Evening Snacks, Side Dish, Snack

CUISINE

  • Indian, Italian

EQUIPMENT

  • Oven
  • Air Fryer

MAIN INGREDIANTS

  • Bread Slices
  • Crushed Garlic
  • Mozzarella Cheese

INGREDIANTS

  • 4 – 5 cloves Garlic, minced
  • 4 tblsp Salted Butter (Amul)
  • 2 tblsps Extra-virgin Olive oil
  • 1 Regular Bread Loaf
  • 3 – 4 tblsp grated Cheese
  • 1 tblsp Oregano
  • 1 tsp Red Chilli Flakes (optional)

How to make cheese garlic bread with regular bread at home

  1. In case you do not have olive oil, then follow this method.
  2. Soften the amul butter and mix the minced garlic in the butter. Keep aside for 30 mins. This will infuse the garlic flavor in the butter.
  3. Brush the garlic butter mixture generously over bread slices. Sprinkle grated cheese generously on the bread slices.
  4. Put the bread slices on a baking sheet and bake at 170 C for 8-10 minutes or till the cheese melts and picks a brown color.
  5. Before serving garnish with oregano and red chilly flakes if using.
  6. Your homemade cheese garlic bread from regular bread slices is ready to be served with soup and pasta.

  • Pasta
  • Pizza
  • Noodles – Hakka Noodles, Chicken Noodles

Reheating Tips

  1. Just put a think layer of butter coat on top the garlic bread and microwave it for 30 seconds.
  2. You can put it in Air Fryer for 4 minutes to have the crisp of the bread back.
  3. Don’t forget to garnish it with fresh basil leaves or parsley
